ABOUT RECOUP

Why Recoup?

Most recovery apps focus on social support or maintaining a streak, but Recoup focuses on organizing information and resources for the individual, giving you more insight and control over your own journey with guilt-free tools. Our relapse impulse tracker allows individuals to record an urge to use quickly and discreetly, and presents actionable analytics with the data entered. The Recoup button is always accessible, so even if the people the consumer trusts are out of reach, they have effective methods of redirection and grounding themselves at their fingertips. Between curating a custom Recoup procedure and the amount of information they have the option to log, individuals are going to get out of it what they put in, but they will never be punished for not using the app, having the urge to relapse, or physically relapsing. This app is designed to work in tandem with therapy and social support and fill in the gaps when those resources arenâ€™t available to give you tools to Recoup whenever you need to.

Development Process

We started with the overall goal to help those currently in substance misuse recovery. Originally, we wanted to create an app that would provide a community for those in recovery and allow them to keep track of their streaks of sobriety. However, a competitive analysis soon proved that solutions like this already existed on the market. After several more design discussions and consulting with potential users, an expert in recovery, responses to our survey, and existing research on the subject of recovery, we reached our final idea: to create an app that allows users to keep track of their more difficult moments in recovery, so that they can look back on them and discover potential patterns, gain insights, and be more reflective on their journey. This is a solution that does not yet exist in the world of technological addiction recovery tools, and we are excited to bring this idea to life. Once we finalized this idea, we began designing wireframes on Figma to visualize our solution. After getting feedback on our wireframes, we created our high-fidelity prototype, which you can view here.

Our Solution

Our solution was a mobile app that provides the following core functionalities:

Impulse Tracking

Users can log an impulse to relapse with just a couple of taps. The app will log the time, severity, and optionally the location of the user's impulse to relapse. Users can then choose to journal about this if they would like to record further detail, either at that moment or at the end of the day.

Recoup Button

When on the risk of relapse, tapping the Recoup button presents customized resources for the user to take a moment to recoup. These resources are customized by the user when setting up the app. Resources can include such things as calling a supportive friend, taking deep breaths, and answering journal prompts.

Journaling

Users can reflect on their day with optional journal prompts and write about any impulses they may have logged.

Mood Tracking

Users can record their mood throughout the day, encouraging them to be mindful of their thoughts, feelings, and mood patterns.

Data Visualization

Users can see the recorded data on their logged impulses and mood tracking, displayed as easily-interpretable graphs to identify any potential patterns and garner insights into their recovery journey.

Home Screen

The home page displays the mood tracking tool, so the user can log their mood right away. It contains a daily journal prompt that is pushed to the user, and there is also a daily affirmation that the user could find motivating for their recovery. The user may also log an impulse straight away from the home page. It is on the home screen for the user to easily access it when needed.

Impulses

The impulse tracker allows users to log whenever they feel an impulse to relapse. It will log the time, inputted severity, and optionally the location of where this impulse was felt. Users can then look back on them to find patterns in when, where, or how severe their impulses to relapse are over time. They may also go back to journal about the impulse later, or in the moment when they feel the impulse, at their convenience.

Recoup Button

Users can customize the resources that they feel would best help them in a moment of crisis in their recovery journey. These resources can include a direct call to a trusted friend or person who can talk them through a severe impulse to relapse, a breathing exercise, a guided meditation, instructions to remove themselves from the immediate environment, journal prompts, and much more. Tapping the recoup button in a moment of crisis will pull up these customized resources for the user.

Mood Tracking

The mood tracking page allows users to log their mood throughout the day. This encourages more mindfulness about their mood, feelings, and behavioral patterns. The logged mood tracking data will be displayed in data visualizations that the user can see at any time.

Who is Recoup for?

The process of recovery is hardly linear. It should instead be thought of as an "upward spiral" model, with the patient going through six stages of change in an iterative manner, learning and improving more and more each time.

The six stages are as follows:

  1. Pre Contemplation
  2. Contemplation
  3. Preparation
  4. Action
  5. Maintenance
  6. Relapse
Note that relapse is a part of the recovery process and does not mean that you have failed. To learn more about the six stages of recovery, click here.

Our solution is aimed for those in the Prepartion, Action, and Maintenance stages. We hope that this app can be an additional tool for users to add to their repertoire of recovery aids, methods and tools. Our solution is not designed to be the one and only key to a successful recovery, nor should it be seen as one, as there is not one single solution to curing addiction nor any other mental health struggle. Our team at Recoup Group wish every one of our users the best of luck, health, and happiness in their recovery journey.

Privacy and Security

The information about the user and the data that the user logs will not be collected, sold, or otherwise used by any third party apps. The data will be encrypted and stored in a cloud database. The communication between the database and the mobile app will also be encrypted. Only the user can view the data they log about themselves. The purposes of this app is solely to serve as a tool for those in recovery, and not for purposes of any kind of monetization.
